## 1. The Nine-Layer Model

Modern AGI research proposes layered self-awareness architectures:
- **Sensor** → Raw input processing
- **Context** → Understanding current state
- **Evaluation** → Assessing quality/outcomes
- **Adversarial** → Testing assumptions
- **Resource** → Managing computational limits
- **Infrastructure** → Core systems health
- **Creative** → Novel connections
- **Priority** → What matters most
- **Urgency** → What needs immediate attention

These map EXACTLY to the Nine Worlds:
- Midgard → Sensor
- Asgard → Context/Planning
- Vanaheim → Evaluation
- Jötunheim → Adversarial
- Svartálfaheim → Resource
- Nidavellir → Infrastructure
- Álfheim → Creative
- Hel → Priority (deferred)
- Muspelheim → Urgency

## 2. Why Nine?

The number nine is significant in Norse cosmology (Odin hung for nine nights, nine worlds on Yggdrasil). But it's also psychologically and computationally meaningful:
- Three levels of consciousness (subconscious → conscious → meta-conscious) × three modalities (input → processing → output) = 9
- Not too few (missing important layers) and not too many (overwhelming complexity)
