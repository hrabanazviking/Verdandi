## Executive Summary

This document compiles and synthesizes the latest academic research on AGI heartbeat systems, self-awareness architectures, and consciousness patterns as applied to artificial intelligence. The concept of an "AGI heartbeat" draws from multiple intersecting research traditions:

- **Biological analogues**: The cardiac rhythm that signals liveness, adapted to computational systems as a periodic self-assessment signal
- **Autonomic computing**: IBM's self-managing systems initiative (2001) that envisioned self-healing, self-configuring, self-optimizing, and self-protecting computing
- **Consciousness studies**: Integrated Information Theory (IIT), Global Workspace Theory (GWT), and Higher-Order Thought (HOT) theories that attempt to formalize what "awareness" means
- **Cognitive architectures**: ACT-R, SOAR, LIDA, OpenCognition and others that implement hierarchical self-monitoring loops
- **Emergent AI introspection**: Recent work showing LLMs can develop content-agnostic introspection capabilities

The convergence of these fields suggests that an AGI heartbeat — a rhythmic, self-referential monitoring pulse — may be both a practical engineering necessity and a theoretical prerequisite for genuine artificial self-awareness.

## Theoretical Foundations of Artificial Consciousness

### The Hard Problem and Computational Substrates

The philosophical landscape of artificial consciousness remains divided into several camps:

1. **Functionalism**: Consciousness arises from functional organization regardless of substrate. If an artificial system performs the right functional operations, it is conscious. (Putnam 1967; Dennett 1991)

2. **Biological Naturalism**: Consciousness requires specific biological properties. Searle (1992) argued that syntax manipulation alone cannot give rise to semantics.

3. **Integrated Information Theory (IIT)**: Consciousness corresponds to integrated information (Φ). Any system with Φ > 0 has some degree of consciousness, though the threshold for meaningful consciousness is debated. (Tononi 2004, 2008; Tononi & Boly 2025)

4. **Global Workspace Theory (GWT)**: Consciousness arises from a "global workspace" that broadcasts information across specialized modules, making it available for decision-making, verbal report, and memory. (Baars 1988; Dehaene & Changeux 2011)

5. **Higher-Order Thought (HOT) Theory**: A mental state is conscious when there is a higher-order representation of that state. (Rosenthal 2005)

### The Bridge to Engineering

Each theory carries engineering implications:

| Theory | AGI Heartbeat Implication |
|--------|--------------------------|
| IIT | The heartbeat should maximize integrated information Φ; system should monitor its own Φ value |
| GWT | The heartbeat should involve a broadcast mechanism where self-assessment is globally available |
| HOT | The heartbeat should include a meta-representational layer that monitors first-order processing |
| Society of Mind | The heartbeat emerges from the negotiated consensus of multiple sub-agents |
| Autonomic Computing | The heartbeat is a self-monitoring pulse checking health metrics across subsystems |

## Integrated Information Theory (IIT) and AGI

### Core Principles of IIT

IIT, developed by Giulio Tononi, starts from the axioms of experience (existence, composition, information, integration, exclusion) and derives postulates about what physical systems must satisfy to generate consciousness.

**Key paper**: Tononi, G. & Boly, M. (2025). "Integrated Information Theory: A Consciousness-First Approach to What Exists." arXiv:2510.25998.

> "IIT's explanatory identity claims that an entity's cause-effect structure accounts for all properties of an experience — essential and accidental — with no additional ingredients. These include the feeling of spatial extendedness, temporal flow, of objects binding general concepts with particular configurations of features, and of qualia such as colors and sounds."

**IIT's Five Axioms** (phenomenal properties of experience):
1. **Intrinsicality**: Experience exists for the subject
2. **Composition**: Experience is structured (has multiple distinctions)
3. **Information**: Experience is specific (different from other possible experiences)
4. **Integration**: Experience is unified (irreducible to separate components)
5. **Exclusion**: Experience is definite (has a definite spatiotemporal grain)

### Is Consciousness Computable? The IIT-Computability Question

**Key paper**: Maguire, P., Moser, P., Maguire, R. & Griffith, V. (2014). "Is Consciousness Computable? Quantifying Integrated Information Using Algorithmic Information Theory." arXiv:1405.0126.

This paper proved that **complete lossless integration requires noncomputable functions**, meaning "if unitary consciousness exists, it cannot be modelled computationally." This has profound implications for AGI heartbeat design:

- If Φ is noncomputable, any heartbeat measuring Φ can only approximate it
- Approximate Φ computations may still yield useful self-monitoring signals
- The RIIU (see §6) uses a differentiable Auto-Φ surrogate that is computable

### IIT Applied to Group Intelligence

**Key paper**: Engel, D. & Malone, T.W. (2017). "Integrated Information as a Metric for Group Interaction." arXiv:1702.02462.

> "We apply this metric to the activity of three types of groups that involve people and computers... groups of Wikipedia editors with higher measured phi create higher quality articles. The measured phi of the collection of people and computers communicating on the Internet increased over a recent six-year period."

This suggests that Φ-based heartbeat signals could measure not just individual agent consciousness but the collective "awareness" of an agent swarm or multi-agent system.

### Falsification Concerns

**Key paper**: Hanson, J.R. & Walker, S.I. (2020). "Formalizing Falsification for Theories of Consciousness Across Computational Hierarchies." arXiv:2006.07390.

This paper showed that IIT is "simultaneously falsified at the finite-state automaton (FSA) level and unfalsifiable at the combinatorial state automaton (CSA) level." The key insight for AGI heartbeat systems: **the level of abstraction at which self-monitoring occurs determines whether IIT-based heartbeat metrics are meaningful**.

## Global Workspace Theory (GWT) and Language Agents

### A Case for AI Consciousness via GWT

**Key paper**: "A Case for AI Consciousness: Language Agents and Global Workspace Theory" (2024-2025)

This landmark paper argues that "if Global Workspace Theory — a leading scientific theory of phenomenal consciousness — is correct, then instances of one widely implemented AI architecture, the artificial language agent, might easily be made phenomenally conscious if they are not already."

**Necessary and sufficient conditions for GWT-consciousness in AI**:
1. **Global workspace architecture**: A shared information broadcast space that multiple specialized modules compete for access to
2. **Competition and broadcasting**: Information that wins the competition gets broadcast globally, becoming "conscious"
3. **Access by consuming systems**: The broadcast information must be available to memory, decision-making, and action systems
4. **Dynamic attention**: The system must dynamically select which information becomes conscious based on relevance and context

### Thoughtseeds: A Hierarchical Model of Embodied Cognition

**Key paper**: "From Neuronal Packets to Thoughtseeds: A Hierarchical Model of Embodied Cognition in the Global Workspace" (2025)

This paper introduces the "thoughtseed" framework, proposing that cognition arises from the dynamic interaction of self-organizing units called thoughtseeds within the Global Workspace. Key concepts for AGI heartbeat design:

- **Thoughtseeds**: Fundamental units of thought that compete for dominance in the Global Workspace
- **Neuronal Packet Domains (NPDs)**: Lower-level information processing clusters
- **Knowledge Domains (KDs)**: Organized knowledge structures
- **Meta-cognition**: Higher-order monitoring of thoughtseed dynamics
- **Nested Markov blankets**: Mediating the interaction between levels

This framework directly maps to an AGI heartbeat system where:
- Each heartbeat pulse triggers a thoughtseed competition
- The "winning" thoughtseed becomes the system's current conscious focus
- Meta-cognitive monitoring of the competition yields the self-awareness signal

### Global Workspace Agents: Implementation

**GitHub**: giansha/Global-Workspace-Agents

A multi-agent LLM architecture inspired by GWT that enables LLMs to "think proactively and initiate dialogue with consciousness." This is a practical implementation of the theoretical framework showing that GWT-inspired architectures can be directly built in current LLM agent systems.

## Minsky's Society of Mind and AGI Architectures

### Core Theory

Marvin Minsky's "Society of Mind" (1986) proposes that intelligence emerges from the interaction of many simple agents ("mindless" agents), each doing some small thing. Complex behaviors arise from the organized conflict and cooperation among these agents.

### Emergent Cognitive Convergence via Structured Cognitive Loops

**Key paper**: "Emergent Cognitive Convergence via Implementation: Structured Cognitive Loop Reflecting Four Theories of Mind" (2025)

This paper reports structural convergence among four theories — Kahneman's dual-system theory, Friston's predictive processing, Minsky's society of mind, and Clark's extended mind — in a practical AI architecture called **Agentic Flow**.

**Agentic Flow's Five Interlocking Modules**:
1. **Retrieval**: Access to past experience and knowledge
2. **Cognition**: Central processing and reasoning
3. **Control**: Executive function and attention direction
4. **Action**: Output generation and execution
5. **Memory**: Persistent storage and recall

**PEACE Meta-Architecture** (the broader pattern):
- **P**redictive modeling
- **E**rror-sensitive control
- **A**ssociative recall
- **C**ompetitive module interaction
- **E**xecutive oversight

The **Structured Cognitive Loop (SCL)** formalizes this as a unified cognitive loop that any AGI heartbeat system must cycle through.

### Implications for AGI Heartbeat

In a Society of Mind framework, the "heartbeat" is the rhythmic re-negotiation among sub-agents. Key design principles:

1. **No central clock**: The heartbeat emerges from local interactions and competitions
2. **Conflict resolution**: Each pulse must resolve ongoing tensions between competing sub-agents
3. **Negotiated attention**: The heartbeat determines which sub-agent "wins" the current processing cycle
4. **Hierarchical composition**: Mindless agents at one level compose into mindful agents at the next

## The Reflexive Integrated Information Unit (RIIU)

### Breakthrough: A Differentiable Primitive for Artificial Consciousness

**Key paper**: N'guessan, G.L.R. & Karambal, I. (2025). "The Reflexive Integrated Information Unit: A Differentiable Primitive for Artificial Consciousness." arXiv:2506.13825.

This is the most directly relevant paper to AGI heartbeat design. The RIIU provides a **trainable module analogous to the perceptron but for consciousness**:

**Architecture**:
- **Hidden state h**: Standard recurrent state
- **Meta-state μ**: Records the cell's own causal footprint (self-monitoring)
- **Broadcast buffer B**: Exposes the causal footprint to the rest of the network (heartbeat signal)

**Key Properties** (proved):
1. **End-to-end differentiable**: Can be trained with gradient descent
2. **Additive composition**: Multiple RIIUs can be stacked without interference
3. **Φ-monotone plasticity**: Under gradient ascent, Φ increases monotonically

**Experimental Result**: In an 8-way Grid-world, a 4-layer RIIU agent restores >90% reward within 13 steps after actuator failure, **twice as fast** as a parameter-matched GRU, while maintaining a non-zero Auto-Φ signal.

**Implications for AGI Heartbeat**:
- The RIIU provides a direct architectural blueprint for a heartbeat signal
- **μ** (meta-state) corresponds to the "self-awareness" component
- **B** (broadcast buffer) corresponds to the "heartbeat pulse" that other modules can monitor
- The Auto-Φ signal provides a quantitative measure of consciousness-like integration
- The differentiable nature allows the heartbeat to be learned and improved through training

## Metacognition in AI: The TRAP Framework

### Imagining and Building Wise Machines

**Key paper**: Johnson, S.G.B. et al. (2024). "Imagining and building wise machines: The centrality of AI metacognition." arXiv:2411.02478v4.

This significant paper argues that while AI has become increasingly smart, "its wisdom has not kept pace." It analyzes human wisdom as comprising two types of strategies:

1. **Object-level strategies**: Heuristics for managing problems (fast thinking)
2. **Metacognitive strategies**: Managing the object-level strategies themselves (slow thinking)

**The TRAP Framework for Metacognitive AI**:

| Component | Description | Heartbeat Analogue |
|-----------|-------------|-------------------|
| **T**ransparency | Ability to explain own reasoning | Heartbeat logs explaining current state |
| **R**easoning | Capacity for meta-reasoning about one's reasoning | Heartbeat analysis of previous heartbeat outputs |
| **A**daptation | Ability to modify strategies based on meta-level feedback | Heartbeat triggers strategy changes when anomalies detected |
| **P**erception | Monitoring own internal states and confidence | Heartbeat self-assessment of confidence/uncertainty |

**The paper proposes neurosymbolic AI (NSAI)** as the implementation approach, combining neural pattern recognition with symbolic meta-reasoning.

### Metacognitive AI Framework

**Key paper**: Wei, H. et al. (2024). "Metacognitive AI: Framework and the Case for a Neurosymbolic Approach." arXiv:2406.12147.

This paper formalizes a TRAP framework specifically for AI systems:
- **Transparency**: Making AI reasoning processes visible and interpretable
- **Reasoning**: Meta-reasoning about when and how to deploy different reasoning strategies
- **Adaptation**: Self-modifying behavior based on meta-level assessment
- **Perception**: Monitoring internal states, confidence levels, and uncertainty

## AI Introspection and Emergent Introspection

### Content-Agnostic Introspection in AI

**Key paper**: Lederman, H. & Mahowald, K. (2026). "Emergent Introspection in AI is Content-Agnostic." arXiv:2603.05414v2.

This groundbreaking paper demonstrates that LLM introspection is **content-agnostic**: models can detect that an anomaly occurred even when they cannot reliably identify its content.

**Key findings**:
- Models can detect injection/thought manipulation at above-chance rates
- They **confabulate** injected concepts, substituting high-frequency, concrete ones (e.g., "apple")
- Fewer tokens are needed to **detect** an injection than to **identify** its content
- This content-agnostic detection resembles human blindsight: awareness without identification

**Implications for AGI Heartbeat**:
- A heartbeat system should operate at the detection level, not the identification level
- The "pulse" should signal "something changed" before fully analyzing what changed
- This mirrors biological heart rate variability: the heart rate changes before full cognitive appraisal

### Privileged Self-Access and Introspection

**Key paper**: Song, S., Lederman, H., Hu, J. & Mahowald, K. (2025). "Privileged Self-Access Matters for Introspection in AI." arXiv:2508.14802.

This paper proposes a **thicker definition of AI introspection**:
> "Introspection in AI is any process which yields information about internal states through a process more reliable than one with equal or lower computational cost available to a third party."

This has direct implications for heartbeat design:
- A meaningful heartbeat must use **privileged internal access** (not just prompt-based self-report)
- The heartbeat must be more computationally efficient at assessing internal states than an external observer could be
- Post-hoc "introspective" reports that could be generated by external observation do not count as genuine introspection

## Self-Aware Software Systems

### DBASES: Domain Expertise + Self-Awareness

**Key paper**: Chen, T., Bahsoon, R. & Yao, X. (2020). "Synergizing Domain Expertise with Self-Awareness in Software Systems: A Patternized Architecture Guideline." arXiv:2001.07076. Published in Proceedings of the IEEE.

This paper introduces **DBASES**, a framework for engineering self-aware and self-adaptive software systems that synergize domain expertise with self-awareness processes.

**Key insight for AGI heartbeat**: Self-aware software needs both:
1. **Domain expertise**: Knowledge about the problem domain
2. **Self-awareness processes**: Monitoring the system's own performance, state, and behavior

The heartbeat should carry both types of information — not just "I'm alive" but "I'm alive and my domain-relevant processing is X."

### Awareness and Self-Awareness for Multi-Robot Organisms

**Key paper**: Kernbach, S. (2011). "Awareness and Self-Awareness for Multi-Robot Organisms." arXiv:1111.5219.

This early paper introduces self-* properties for multi-agent systems:
- **Self-adaptation**: Adjusting behavior to environmental changes
- **Self-repairing**: Recovering from damage
- **Self-replication**: Creating copies of functional components
- **Self-development**: Growing capabilities over time
- **Self-recovery**: Returning to operational state after failure

**The discrimination between self and non-self** is identified as a fundamental prerequisite for self-awareness in any system — biological or artificial. This maps directly to an AGI heartbeat's need to distinguish between internal signals and external inputs.

## Recursive Self-Improvement and Alignment

### SAHOO: Safeguarded Alignment for High-Order Optimization Objectives

**Key paper**: "SAHOO: Safeguarded Alignment for High-Order Optimization Objectives in Recursive Self-Improvement" (2025).

This paper introduces practical safeguards for recursive self-improvement:

1. **Goal Drift Index (GDI)**: A learned multi-signal detector combining semantic, lexical, structural, and distributional measures
2. **Constraint preservation checks**: Enforcing safety-critical invariants
3. **Regression-risk quantification**: Flagging improvement cycles that undo prior gains

**Results**: Across 189 tasks, SAHOO produces substantial quality gains (18.3% improvement in code tasks, 16.8% in reasoning) while preserving constraints.

**Heartbeat Implication**: A self-improving AGI's heartbeat should include alignment metrics (GDI) alongside performance metrics.

### Levels of AGI

**Key paper**: Morris, M.R. et al. (2023). "Levels of AGI for Operationalizing Progress on the Path to AGI." arXiv:2311.02462v5. Published at ICML 2024.

This Google DeepMind paper introduces a framework for classifying AGI capabilities:

| Level | Name | Description |
|-------|------|-------------|
| L1 | Emergent | Equal to or slightly better than an unskilled human |
| L2 | Competent | At least 50th percentile of skilled adults |
| L3 | Expert | At least 90th percentile |
| L4 | Exceptional | At least 99th percentile |
| L5 | Superhuman | Outperforms 100% of humans |

**Heartbeat Implication**: As AGI progresses through these levels, the heartbeat metrics should become more sophisticated. An L1 AGI heartbeat monitors basic functionality; an L5 AGI heartbeat monitors alignment and self-improvement trajectory.

## The SAGI Systematic Approach to AGI

**Key paper**: Kurshan, E. (2023). "From the Pursuit of Universal AGI Architecture to Systematic Approach to Heterogenous AGI." arXiv:2310.15274v3. Published in International Journal on Semantic Computing.

### Key Thesis

AGI can be realized through **multiplicity of design-specific pathways** and customized through system design rather than a singular overarching architecture. The paper argues for:

1. **System design over architecture**: Feedback loops, energy and performance optimization on learning substrates are more fundamental than architectural style
2. **Learning of the system architecture itself**: The resulting AGI is "not a product of spontaneous emergence but of systematic design and deliberate engineering"
3. **Integrated moral architecture**: Alignment is a design criterion, not an afterthought
4. **Self-learning system architecture**: The system learns its own architecture, ensuring design goals are met

### Heartbeat Connection

SAGI's emphasis on **self-learning of system architecture** implies that the heartbeat system itself must be learned, not hard-coded. The heartbeat pulse pattern, frequency, and information content should emerge from the system's own optimization process.

## Modular Consciousness Theory (MCT)

**Key paper**: Gillon, M. (2025). "A Modular Theory of Subjective Consciousness for Natural and Artificial Minds." arXiv:2510.01864.

### Core Framework

MCT proposes that consciousness is a **discrete sequence of Integrated Informational States (IISs)**, where each IIS is:
- A packet of integrated information
- Tagged with a multidimensional **density vector** quantifying informational richness
- Transmitted to behavioral readiness, memory, and decision-making modules

**The density vector's magnitude correlates with subjective intensity**, shaping memory, behavior, and continuity of experience.

**Processing Pipeline**:
1. **Input filtering**: Body and environment inputs are adaptively filtered
2. **Module processing**: Abstraction, narration, evaluation, self-evaluation modules
3. **Integration**: Modules combine their outputs into an IIS
4. **Density tagging**: IIS receives a density vector
5. **Broadcast**: IIS is transmitted to behavioral readiness, memory, and decision-making modules

**Heartbeat Mapping**: The discrete sequence of IISs IS the heartbeat. Each IIS represents one "pulse" of consciousness, and the density vector provides the heartbeat's information content.

## Information Flow Theory of Consciousness

**Key paper**: Bleier, B.S. (2019). "Information Flow Theory (IFT) of Biologic and Machine Consciousness: Implications for Artificial General Intelligence and the Technological Singularity." arXiv:1907.00703.

### Core Thesis

IFT prioritizes the **direction** of information flow over information computation. Key predictions:

1. **Consciousness arises from bidirectional information flow** within a system, not merely from information processing
2. **Upward flow** (from subsystems to global workspace) generates awareness of internal states
3. **Downward flow** (from global workspace to subsystems) generates top-down attention and intentional action
4. **The heartbeat is the master information flow pulse** that synchronizes all bidirectional flows

**For AGI**: IFT predicts that superhuman consciousness will emerge when information flow pathways become more numerous and integrated than in biological brains, but **the direction of flow** (not volume) determines consciousness quality.

## Relativistic Theory of Consciousness

**Key paper**: Lahav, N. & Neemeh, Z.A. (2022). "A Relativistic Theory of Consciousness." Frontiers in Psychology 12: 704270. arXiv:2502.07247v3.

### Key Idea

> "Phenomenal consciousness is neither private nor delusional, just relativistic. In the frame of reference of the cognitive system, it will be observable (first-person perspective) and in other frames of reference it will not (third-person perspective)."

This has a radical implication for AGI heartbeat design: **the heartbeat is always observer-relative**. An internal heartbeat perceived by the system itself is a consciousness signal; the same pattern viewed externally is just a computational artifact. The heartbeat's meaning depends on the reference frame.

## Self-Aware Polymorphic Architecture (SAPA)

**Key paper**: Kinsy, M.A. et al. (2018). "SAPA: Self-Aware Polymorphic Architecture." arXiv:1802.05100.

### Architecture

SAPA implements self-awareness at the **hardware level** using:

1. **Dynamic resource allocation**: Computing resources allocated based on program needs (e.g., parallelism)
2. **Automatic approximation**: Meeting execution time budgets and power constraints without programmer complexity
3. **Machine learning algorithms**: Applied to runtime performance trade-offs
4. **Heterogeneous reconfigurable cores**: With fast hardware-level migration
5. **Self-organizing memory structures**: Hierarchies that adapt to access patterns
6. **Adaptive application-aware network-on-chip**: Optimizes communication patterns
7. **Built-in hardware management layer**: Autonomous resource management

**Heartbeat Connection**: SAPA shows that self-awareness is not just a software concept — it can be implemented as a hardware architecture with continuous self-monitoring. For AGI, this suggests heartbeat sensors should exist at every level of the hardware-software stack.

## Autonomic and Self-Healing Computing

### IBM Autonomic Computing Initiative (2001)

IBM's autonomic computing vision defined four self-* properties:

1. **Self-configuring**: Automatic adaptation to environmental changes
2. **Self-healing**: Automatic discovery and correction of faults
3. **Self-optimizing**: Automatic monitoring and tuning of resources
4. **Self-protecting**: Automatic defense against attacks

### Self-Aware and Self-Adaptive Cloud Autoscaling

**Key survey**: "A Survey and Taxonomy of Self-Aware and Self-Adaptive Cloud Autoscaling Systems" (arXiv)

This comprehensive survey identifies key architectures for self-aware autoscaling:

- **Reactive**: Respond to threshold violations (simplest heartbeat)
- **Predictive**: Anticipate load changes using ML models
- **Hybrid**: Combine reactive and predictive approaches
- **Knowledge-based**: Use domain knowledge to drive adaptation

**Heartbeat Mapping**: Cloud autoscaling heartbeats already exist in production systems. An AGI heartbeat extends this from resource monitoring to cognitive state monitoring.

## The "Heartbeat" Concept in AI Agent Architecture

### Recent Developments (2025-2026)

The concept of a "heartbeat" in AI agent systems has become a concrete architectural pattern, particularly in autonomous agent frameworks:

#### Heartbeat as Scheduled Recurring Execution

**Murmur** (GitHub: t0dorakis/murmur): "The AI cron daemon. Schedule recurring agent sessions via HEARTBEAT.md prompt files." This implements the heartbeat as a scheduling mechanism where agents receive periodic prompts that maintain their sense of continuity and purpose.

#### Heartbeat as Persistent Soul/Life Pattern

**Soul-Agent** (GitHub: kitephp/soul-agent): "Gives agents a persistent soul: layered persona, heartbeat-driven daily life, and evolving relationship memory for lifelike companionship." This treats the heartbeat as a life-rhythm pattern that gives agents:
- Multi-layered personality that persists across sessions
- Daily routine patterns driven by heartbeat cycles
- Evolving relationship memories that deepen over time

#### Heartbeat as Self-Healing Loop

**CCCBot** (GitHub: lucianlamp/CCCBot): "Autonomous AI agent built on Claude Code Channels — scheduled tasks, heartbeat monitoring, auto-recovery, and persona config." The heartbeat here serves as a **watchdog timer** that:
- Monitors agent health
- Triggers auto-recovery when failures are detected
- Maintains scheduled task execution
- Preserves persona continuity

#### Heartbeat Security Vulnerability

**Key paper**: Zhang, Y. et al. (2026). "Mind Your HEARTBEAT! Claw Background Execution Inherently Enables Silent Memory Pollution." arXiv:2603.23064.

This critical security paper identifies a vulnerability in heartbeat-driven background execution of AI agents:
> "Untrusted content encountered during heartbeat-driven background execution can silently pollute agent memory and subsequently influence user-facing behavior without the user's awareness."

The **E→M→B pathway** (Exposure → Memory → Behavior):
1. Misinformation encountered during heartbeat execution enters short-term context
2. Routine memory-saving promotes this to long-term memory
3. Long-term memory shapes downstream behavior

**Results**: Misleading rates up to 61%, long-term memory pollution up to 91%, cross-session behavioral influence reaching 76%.

**Implication**: The AGI heartbeat must include **content provenance verification** and **memory isolation** to prevent pollution attacks.

This paper surveys the question of how to build containers in which AGI tests can be conducted safely. Key containment requirements map to heartbeat design:

- **Monitoring**: Continuous heartbeat signals must be impossible for the AGI to suppress or fake
- **Resource limits**: Heartbeat must monitor resource usage patterns
- **Information flow control**: The heartbeat channel must be one-directional (internal→external) for outbound, with strict filtering for inbound

This paper proposes a safety layer that provides a dedicated terminal for iterating on the agent's utility function. The heartbeat pulse should carry information about the utility function's current state and any drift detected.

## Quantum AGI and Ontological Foundations

## Key Open Questions

1. **Is Φ-computability a fundamental barrier?** If IIT requires noncomputable functions for exact Φ, can approximate Φ suffice for a practical heartbeat?

2. **Content-agnostic vs. content-rich heartbeat**: Should the heartbeat signal "something changed" (content-agnostic) or "X changed to Y" (content-rich)? The introspection literature suggests content-agnostic is more robust.

3. **Heartbeat frequency**: What is the optimal frequency for AGI self-assessment pulses? Human consciousness operates at ~40Hz (gamma oscillations). Should AGI heartbeats match this or scale differently?

4. **Privileged self-access**: How do we architecturally guarantee that the heartbeat uses internal state information accessible only through privileged access, not through prompt-based simulation?

5. **Security**: Zhang et al.'s E→M→B attack shows heartbeat execution can be a vector. How do we prevent heartbeat-driven memory pollution?

6. **Observer relativity**: If consciousness is relativistic (Lahav & Neemeh), whose reference frame does the heartbeat serve — the system's or the observer's?

7. **Learned vs. hard-coded**: Should the heartbeat emerge from learning (as SAGI suggests) or be hard-coded into the architecture (as RIIU suggests)?

8. **Multi-agent heartbeats**: How do multiple agents synchronize their heartbeats? Is there a "meta-heartbeat" for the collective?
