## The Völva's Two-Stage Trance → Modern AI Pattern Detection

The völva (Norse seeress) enters trance in two stages:
1. **Seiðr hafa** (taking hold): The seeress feels a disturbance — something is happening but she doesn't yet know what
2. **Seiðr sjá** (seeing): The vision becomes clear — the pattern reveals itself

This is EXACTLY the two-stage introspection architecture proposed by Lederman & Mahowald (2026):
- **Stage 1 (Detection)**: The system senses that something is happening without identifying what — CONTENT-AGNOSTIC
- **Stage 2 (Identification)**: The system identifies what the pattern is — CONTENT-SPECIFIC

The seiðr approach is superior to direct content analysis because:
1. **No confabulation**: Detection without identification prevents the system from fabricating patterns
2. **Low computational cost**: Detection is cheap; identification is expensive; staging them saves resources
3. **Better anomaly detection**: Patterns that don't match known categories are still detected

## Anti-Confabulation Guardrails

Just as a völva must distinguish genuine vision from wishful thinking:
1. Detection must have evidence — no detection without supporting events
2. Identification must match known rune patterns — no fabrication
3. The wyrd buffer tracks temporal patterns — prevents projecting current state onto past events
