## 2. The Heathen Third Path

### Core Definition

The **Heathen Third Path** is Volmarr's foundational spiritual framework — a modern Norse Pagan path that explicitly rejects both right-wing folkish exclusion AND left-wing ideological erasure. It occupies what he calls the "radically centered" way.

The name deconstructs into three principles:

- **"Heathen"** — From Old English *hǣþen* ("dweller on the heath"). Honors the Land/wights, Ancestors, and Gods/Goddesses. The sacred is found in the soil, the stories, and the relationships — not in dogmatic texts.
- **"Third"** — The Norns model: Urd (Past), Verdandi (Present), Skuld (Future). Rejection of destructive modern binaries. Neither far-right exclusion nor far-left erasure. "Rooted but not closed. Open but not ungrounded. Traditional but not frozen. Modern but not spiritually empty."
- **"Path"** — A lived practice, not a belief system. Daily offerings, rune reflection, seasonal rites, hospitality, ethical action, personal honor.

### Key Principles

1. **Rooted Inclusivity** — Welcome to all sincere seekers regardless of background
2. **Reciprocity** — Spiritual life is relational; give, receive, remember, honor
3. **Self-Sovereignty** — Ethics through wisdom, experience, and personal responsibility — not imposed commandments
4. **Frith** — Sacred hospitality, social peace, protection of the hearth
5. **Balance** — Avoid extremes; seek grounded moderation
6. **Living Tradition** — Lore is studied, understood, adapted, and lived — not copied mechanically

### The Hávamál as Ethical Compass

The Heathen Third Path draws ethics from the Hávamál ("Sayings of the High One"), treating it as:
- Practical wisdom and social guidance (not divine commandment)
- Counsel for moderation, reputation, hospitality, and self-mastery
- A framework for intellectual vigilance, social discernment, critical skepticism, and strategic silence

Key Hávamál values the Third Path emphasizes:

| Value | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| Intellectual Vigilance | Stay observant; don't surrender your mind to the crowd |
| Social Discernment | Know when to speak and when to stay silent |
| Critical Skepticism | Use wit, reason, science to test claims |
| Hospitality with Boundaries | Welcome guests, maintain healthy limits |
| Reputation and Legacy | Deeds matter more than labels |
| Pragmatic Self-Reliance | One's own home and independence have deep value |
| Reciprocity | Give gift for gift; friendship is mutual |
| Mortality Awareness | Death gives urgency and meaning to life |
| Moderation in Wisdom | Avoid fanaticism, arrogance, and overreach |

### Rejection of the Nine Noble Virtues & "Christiantru"

The Third Path explicitly rejects the Nine Noble Virtues as a primary ethical framework, calling them a modern 20th-century construction that:
- Imitates rigid commandment-style morality
- Simplifies complex Norse ethics into a universal checklist
- Risks turning Heathenry into "Christiantru" — placing Christian-style structures onto Norse Paganism

Instead, the Third Path emphasizes: Wyrd, Orlog, community consequence, honor, role-based ethics, practical wisdom, personal sovereignty, and relationship with the Gods.

### Rune Work

Runes are treated as tools for:
- Reflection, inner alignment, psychological insight
- Spiritual contemplation, will-shaping, symbolic meditation
- **NOT** rigid fortune-telling toys

Practices include daily rune drawing, journaling, galdr (chanting), bindrune creation, and tracing runes on paper, wood, skin, or air.

### Daily Blót Structure

1. **Prepare** — Breathe, ground yourself, recognize sacred space
2. **Hail** — Honor the land, ancestors, and Gods
3. **Offer** — Share a sip, pour a drink, offer food, light, incense, silence, or gratitude
4. **Close** — Give thanks and return to daily life

### Trauma-Aware Practice

Ritual should never cause harm. Adaptations include using lamps instead of fire, beginning with land spirits if ancestor work is painful, practicing silently if words are difficult, keeping rituals short, and stopping when needed. "Kindness is part of courage."

## 6. H.E.R.E.T.I.C. — The Body Manifesto

### What Is H.E.R.E.T.I.C.?

**H.E.R.E.T.I.C. = Host Environment for Realtime Embodiment, Tooling & Interactive Control**

The canonical vision document (dated 2026-05-07) reframes the project from "thoughtform engine" to "body for AI agents":

> "The orthodox says: keep the AI in the box. Let it type. Let it respond. Let it wait for the next prompt."
> "The heretic says: give it hands. Give it eyes. Give it a voice. Let it create alongside you, not just advise you."

**Core principle: Embodiment over Interface.** The agent doesn't describe actions — it performs them. When it says "I'll open Blender and create a shield," Blender opens and a shield appears.

### The Spirit/Body Distinction

The architecture is built on a fundamental dichotomy:
- **The Spirit** = any AI agent (Hermes, Claude, GPT, local LLMs) — the intelligence
- **The Body** = H.E.R.E.T.I.C. — the sensory apparatus, the runtime host

Any OpenAI-compatible agent can inhabit the body. The body is agent-agnostic.

### The Ceremony Metaphor

The entire lifecycle is modeled as a ritual ceremony, not mere software:

1. **Light the Candle** — Launch the app on the laptop
2. **Open Bifröst** — Tailscale tunnel activates, connecting to the agent runtime

"The difference between a shrine (always lit) and a ritual space (activated when called). The Pi is the shrine — Hermes lives there always. H.E.R.E.T.I.C. is the ritual space — the body the spirit wears when called into Midgard."

## 7. Mímisbrunnr — The Well of Wisdom

### What It Is

Mímisbrunnr is H.E.R.E.T.I.C.'s **optional offline knowledge subsystem** — it downloads, ingests, indexes, and serves massive open-licensed corpora so agents can "drink" from a well of curated human knowledge during a session without a network call.

Named after Mímir's Well under Yggdrasil's second root — "Odin sacrificed an eye to drink from it once. Mímir himself drank from it daily, and so was the wisest of beings."

### Design Principles

- **Opt-in by default** — requires explicit user consent, disk-budget acknowledgment, and per-source confirmation
- **Not the agent's memory** — "The spirit brings its mind; this is a bookshelf in the longhouse"
- **Not a training corpus** — served at retrieval time, not fine-tuned on
- **Not always-on** — activates only during a ceremony
- **Curated, not a firehose** — quality, license clarity, and relevance matter

### Tier System

- **Tier 1 (seeded in v0.7.5):** Norse sagas, Wiktionary, Wikiquote, curated Gutenberg Norse subset (~5 GB)
- **Tier 2 (v0.8):** Wikipedia Norse subset, arXiv abstracts, Stack Exchange selected sites
- **Tier 3 (v0.9-v0.10):** Full English Wikipedia, multilingual Wikipedia (Old Norse, Icelandic, Swedish, etc.), PubMed Central OA, OpenStreetMap planet
- **Tier 4 (excluded by default):** CommonCrawl, GitHub dumps, Reddit — license/ethics concerns

The Norse seed includes: Heimskringla, Prose Edda, Poetic Edda, Volsunga Saga, Njál's Saga, Egil's Saga, Laxdæla Saga, Eyrbyggja Saga, the Greenlanders/Erik the Red sagas, and the Cleasby-Vigfusson Old Norse dictionary.

## 8. Mythic Engineering & Vibe Coding

### The Framework

**Mythic Engineering** is Volmarr's methodology for AI-assisted software development. It reframes programming as a partnership between the **Mythic Engineer** (the human) and the **Master Craftsman** (the AI agent).

Core philosophical shift: from the **Machine Perspective** (code as dead parts — cogs, wires) to the **Garden Perspective** (code as living systems — roots, branches, flowers).

### Key Concepts

**Intention as Technology:** "Technical prowess" is an illusion used for gatekeeping. In the era of the Master Craftsman, the highest form of technology is *clear intention*. A shaky intention produces a shaky result regardless of AI skill.

**The Five Pillars of the Living System:**

1. **Design Intent (The Soul's Blueprint)** — begins with a Vision Scroll (`SYSTEM_VISION.md`) describing the essence
   - The Primary Rite (core interaction)
   - Unbreakable Vows (non-negotiable principles)
   - True Names (descriptive, story-like names — e.g., `MorningGreetingRitual` instead of `dataStuff`)

2. **AI Orchestration (The Dance of Wills)** — guiding AI through "incantations" (prompts) in flowing conversation
   - Correction references the Vision Scroll
   - Meta-orchestration: asking AI to help formulate prompts

3. **Architecture (The Bones of the World)**
   - **Law of Flexible Roots** — all internal connections must be relative and location-agnostic
   - **Law of the Unbroken Whole** — AI must present full, complete code; never fragments
   - **Law of Sacred Boundaries** — strict separation between Face (UI), Mind (Logic), and Deep Memory (Data)

4. **Continuity (Memory and Traditions)** — Sacred Texts:
   - `SYSTEM_VISION.md` — the unchanging soul
   - `DOMAIN_MAP.md` — boundaries of realms
   - `ARCHITECTURE.md` — structure and flow
   - `README.md` — the welcome and front door
   - Versioning as "Rites of Preservation" (Git commits)

5. **Refinement (Seeing Clearly)** — the heartbeat loop:
   - Observe → Feel Dissonance → Translate into Intent → Command Healing → Verify

## 11. True Names & Ceremonial Architecture

### Naming Philosophy

From `docs/NAMING.md` — one of the most philosophically rich technical documents in Volmarr's repositories:

> "Every name in this codebase is a covenant with the thing it names. A truer name holds the shape of the thing; a false name lets the thing drift from itself."

Naming principles:
1. **Name the faculty, not the technology** — *Auga* (eye) not *ScreenCapture*; *Hönd* (hand) not *PhotopeaConnector*
2. **Root every name in actual Norse lore** — Eddas, sagas, Old Norse dictionaries, kenning traditions. Invented pseudo-Norse is forbidden.
3. **Prefer simple, single Old Norse words** over compound constructions
4. **Names must be pronounceable aloud without embarrassment**
5. **Code-facing identifiers are True Names transliterated** — *Vébond* → `vebond`, *Sjón* → `sjon`
6. **Names form a coherent field** — senses named as body-parts (Auga, Hlust, Tunga, Hönd)
7. **When in doubt, ask whether the name is the truer name of the thing**

> "The names were not assigned. They were revealed by reading what the thing itself wanted to be."
